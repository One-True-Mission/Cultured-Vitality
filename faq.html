Most probiotics contain five or six bacterial strains. Some include pre-biotics, most don't. Cultured Vitality uses a blend of over 300 beneficial microbes, paired with pre-biotics that help them actually take hold in your gut.

The strains were chosen specifically to encourage the body to release certain enzymes. Those enzymes support your body's natural production of a protein called GcMAF, which helps your body use vitamin D more efficiently. In short, Cultured Vitality helps your body get more out of the vitamin D you're already taking in.

When vitamin D is working properly, it does more than support the immune system. It also supports mood, blood sugar balance, detoxification, and mental clarity.

The daily amount is small (one tablespoon, twice a day). Cultured Vitality is made only with goat milk or A2 cow milk, both of which contain A2 casein. Most people allergic to dairy are reacting to A1 casein or lactose, not A2. On top of that, most of the casein is removed during the culturing process.

If dairy is still a no for you, there are two options. First, try it topically. Yes, on your skin. Customers have reported real benefits using it like a face cream or stomach lotion. Second, grab the DIY kit and make it yourself with a non-dairy milk of your choice.

Most people don't report any. A small number get a mild headache, feel a little queasy, or use the bathroom more often in the first week. These are signs that your body is detoxing, which is a good thing.

If you do feel these symptoms, cut your dose down. Slower detox means less discomfort. Build the dose back up gradually.

The people who have the toughest time tend to be those who keep eating heavy junk and sugary drinks while starting the protocol. Your body is trying to clear toxins out, and if more keep coming in, it can get overwhelmed.

Once people stick with it, almost everyone reports significantly better energy, sleep, and digestion within the first few weeks.

For a real microbiome reset, plan on a 90-day cycle. That window gives the cultures time to fully take hold and repopulate your gut flora.

If you're dealing with something specific, talk to your healthcare provider. You can also email Brittany directly at info@culturedvitality.org with questions about your situation.

Always check with your healthcare provider before starting any new diet or supplement. With that said, the diet that pairs best with this protocol is low in added sugar, high in leafy greens and fiber, and avoids eating after 8 p.m. so your body has time to digest overnight.

For best results, vitamin D is often recommended at higher doses (around 10,000 IU is common). Vitamin D can be overdosed, so please confirm dosage with your doctor first. Vitamin K typically pairs with D, so a D3/K2 combo supplement keeps it simple.

Ideally, take Cultured Vitality on an empty stomach (morning before food, and evening before bed). If that doesn't fit your schedule, just take it daily. The protocol is meant to be simple, not stressful.

Antibiotics and certain medications can interfere with probiotic potency. If you're on a course of antibiotics, talk to your physician. You may want to wait until you finish before starting.

Avoid other probiotics while on Cultured Vitality (other supplements, yogurt, kefir, kombucha, fermented foods). Other cultures will compete in the gut and dilute the effect.
